2. Use of Cookies

Our website, ovo-games.github.io, uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance user experience, analyze site traffic, and deliver personalized content and advertisements. Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, mobile, tablet) by your web browser when you visit a website.

We use both first-party and third-party cookies:

  • First-party cookies are set directly by our website to recognize your preferences and improve site functionality.
  • Third-party cookies may be set by services we use, such as Google AdSense and analytics providers, to deliver targeted advertisements, track ad performance, and gather anonymous visitor statistics.

Examples of how cookies are used on our site include:

  • Remembering your preferences or settings
  • Serving relevant and personalized ads through services like Google AdSense
  • Measuring the effectiveness of advertisements
  • Understanding how users interact with different parts of the website

By using our site, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy. However, you can manage or disable cookies at any time by adjusting your browser settings. Most web browsers allow users to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or be notified when cookies are being placed. Please note that disabling cookies may impact certain features or functionalities of our site.

To learn more about how Google uses cookies in advertising, visit Google's Advertising Policy.

5. GDPR Compliance (For EU Visitors)

If you are accessing ovo-games.github.io from the European Union (EU), you have certain rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) regarding your personal data. While we do not intentionally collect any personally identifiable information, we want to ensure that you are aware of your rights and how to exercise them.

The following are your rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Access: You have the right to request a copy of any personal data we may hold about you, including information regarding how it is processed.
  • Right to Rectification: If any personal data we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request that it be corrected or updated.
  • Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): You have the right to request the deletion of any personal data we may hold about you, subject to certain conditions.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You have the right to request that we limit the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability: You have the right to request that we transfer your personal data to another service provider in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object: You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data for specific purposes, such as direct marketing.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: If you have previously provided consent for any data processing, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the legality of the processing based on your consent prior to withdrawal.

If you wish to exercise any of these rights or have concerns about how we handle your personal data, please contact us using the information provided on our Contact Page. We will respond to your request within the time frame stipulated by the GDPR (usually one month). If you feel that your rights have been violated, you have the right to file a complaint with your local data protection authority.

We take your privacy seriously and will take all reasonable steps to protect your personal information. However, please note that we do not knowingly collect or store personal data without your explicit consent. Any non-personal data we collect (such as cookies and analytics data) is used solely for the purpose of improving user experience and site performance.

6. CCPA Compliance (For California Residents)

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), residents of California have certain rights regarding their personal information. Although we do not sell any personal data, we respect your privacy rights and want to provide full transparency on how you can exercise your rights under the CCPA.

The following are your rights under the CCPA:

  • Right to Know: You have the right to request details about the categories and specific pieces of personal information that we have collected about you, as well as the purposes for which the information is used.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request that we delete any personal information we may have collected about you, subject to certain exceptions (such as where we need to retain the information for legal or business purposes).
  • Right to Opt-Out of the Sale of Personal Information: While we do not sell personal data, you have the right to opt out of any future sale of your personal information (if applicable in the future).
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: You have the right not to face discrimination for exercising any of your CCPA rights. We will not deny you services, charge you different prices, or provide a lower level of service based on your exercise of these rights.

If you wish to exercise any of your CCPA rights, please contact us through the information provided on our Contact Page. We will respond to your request in accordance with CCPA timelines (usually within 45 days). Please note that, in some cases, we may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

In the event of any concern regarding our data practices, California residents may also file a complaint with the California Attorney General's Office. For more information on CCPA rights, you can visit the California Attorney General's official website at https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring that your personal data is handled securely and responsibly. While we do not sell your personal information, we strive to be transparent and ensure that all your rights under CCPA are respected.
